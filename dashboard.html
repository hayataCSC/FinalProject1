*The correlation coefficients are calculated over a ten-year-period with 1970 data corresponding to the period between 1960 and 1969. For the correlation between money supply and inflation rate, I created one year gap between the two variables. For example, the 1990 data corresponds to the correlation between the annual changes in money supply in the 1980-to-1989 period and inflation rates in the 1981-to-1990 period. This one-year gap is to accommodate the time necessary for a change in money supply to surface as a change in the consumer price index.
